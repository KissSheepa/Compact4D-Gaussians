## Abstract

<div className="text-justify">
3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a pioneering approach for novel view synthesis, catalyzing significant research into dynamic scene representation. However, modeling dynamic scenes with 4DGS requires a large number of Gaussians to capture temporal dynamics, resulting in massive storage, which hinders practical applicability. To address this challenge, we propose a novel compaction framework specifically designed for 4DGS. Our key insight is to decouple the compaction strategies for static and dynamic components, which balances storage efficiency and temporal fidelity. Specifically, we introduce a Dynamic-Aware Adaptive Sparsification strategy, which incorporates a motion saliency attention mechanism into the optimization-driven pruning process, assigning higher retention priority to motion-dependent Gaussians to prevent detail loss. Furthermore, we devise a Static-Aware Merging technique to consolidate spatially redundant primitives specifically within the static background, strictly preserving dynamic elements to ensure temporal consistency. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method significantly reduces storage requirements for dynamic scenes while maintaining high-quality rendering performance.
</div>

</HighlightedSection>

## Method Overview

<Figure>
  <Picture slot="figure" src={frameworkImg} alt="Overview of our proposed framework" />
  <Fragment slot="caption">
    <div className="text-justify">
      **Overview of our proposed framework.** 
      The pipeline consists of three main phases:
      (Top Row) The training pipeline begins with Initialization & Decomposition (A), where primitives are classified into dynamic and static groups. Dynamic primitives undergo deformation (B), while all primitives are subjected to standard density control (C).
      (Bottom Left) The Dynamic-Aware Sparsification Module (D) is introduced to compact the scene globally. It employs an ADMM-based optimization where the sparsifying step is guided by a dynamic-aware weight $w$, ensuring critical dynamic primitives are preserved.
      (Bottom Right) The Static-Background Merging Module (E) further compresses the model as a post-processing step. It selectively filters static primitives and merges redundant ones based on spatial and appearance similarity within grid cells.
      Finally, the compacted primitives are fine-tuned to produce the rendered images.
